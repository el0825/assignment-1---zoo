Koala

Koalas are marsupials native to eastern Australia, instantly recognizable by their round faces, fluffy ears, and spoon-shaped noses. Despite being called “koala bears,” they are not bears at all. Koalas feed almost exclusively on eucalyptus leaves, which are low in nutrition and require a specialized digestive system to detoxify. Because of this poor-energy diet, koalas spend up to 20 hours each day sleeping or resting in the branches of eucalyptus trees. They play an important ecological role by pruning trees and spreading nutrients through their droppings.
